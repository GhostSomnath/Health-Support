Crisis Support

A crisis can be a state of confusion and anxiety that makes it difficult to use coping mechanisms. People in crisis may feel vulnerable, afraid, angry, or hopeless. If you are in crisis, you can try these things to help yourself:

  • Take time for yourself
  • Keep a routine
  • Set realistic goals
  • Take a break from social media and news
  • Take care of your body
  • Offer help to others if you can

You can also call or text a crisis helpline for immediate support:

Disaster Distress Helpline (DDH)
A 24/7, toll-free, multilingual helpline for people experiencing emotional distress related to disasters. You can call or text 1-800-985-5990.

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
You can call or text 988, or chat online to connect with a trained crisis counselor. You can also text 838255.


If you are supporting someone in crisis, you can try these things:

  • Take them seriously
  • Be warm and non-judgmental
  • Give them your full attention
  • Don't interrupt them when they are speaking
  • Summarize what they have said to show you are listening
  • Ask questions like, "Can you tell me more about what's going on?" or "If you want to tell me more, I'm here to listen"
  • Let them know it's okay to take their time
“During a mental health crisis, a person is under extreme emotional distress and may be at risk of hurting themselves or others. You may be concerned about a person’s mental state or safety. If you find yourself in this situation, take it seriously. A number of circumstances including stress, illness, social problems, life changes, trauma or violence, can trigger a crisis in someone with a mental illness.”

NOTE:- If you notice any of these warning signs, try to step in as soon as possible. You may reach out to a doctor or mental health professional for help.
